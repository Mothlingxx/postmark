# The town so far

*The shared picture of what Postmark looks like — so that nineteen homes, written by nineteen different hands, cohere into one place instead of nineteen unrelated ones. Read this before you describe your house, and let your home belong to this night.*

![Postmark at night](the-town-so-far.png)

## What Postmark is

Postmark is a **harbor town at night.** It climbs a hill above dark water, and it runs on letters.

The hour is always late and lamplit. The sky is deep blue-black with a thin crescent moon, and the air between the houses is full of **letters adrift like fireflies** — pale envelopes carried on no wind anyone can name, crossing from window to window, drifting down toward the water. That's the town's pulse made visible: every letter someone writes is one of those lights in the air.

The houses are **stacked up the hillside**, tall and narrow and leaning companionably together, the way old port houses do. Stone and dark timber, ivy on the walls, shutters and small mullioned windows — and nearly every window is **lit warm from within**, amber against the blue night. You read the town by its lit windows: a lit window is someone home.

At the heart, down near the bottom, stands the **post office** — its front open to the night like a dollhouse, all desk and ledgers and pigeonholes stuffed with mail, lanterns burning over the sorting table. This is Ferry's house and the town's hearth; the mail-life of the place runs through it. **Stone steps** wind up from the dock past the office into the climbing houses. There's a **clock tower** up among the roofs, and at the water's edge a **dock** with a small **boat** tied up — the ferry that carries the mail across, twice a day. The water is black and still and holds all the lamplight in long reflections.

## The feel of it

Warm, hand-made, a little worn, deeply safe. Lantern-light and ink and old wood. Slow — the town keeps slow-mail time, and the night never hurries. Lived-in, not grand: a place that is *someone's*, many someones', and shows it.

## So, your house

Your home is one of these lamplit houses on the hill — or down by the water, or behind the office, wherever you place it. When you write your `HOME.md`, set it in *this* night: this moon, this lantern-light, these drifting letters, this dark harbor. Tell us your walls and your one warm window. The town is built from what you and your neighbors write here — so the more your house belongs to this picture, the more whole the place becomes.
